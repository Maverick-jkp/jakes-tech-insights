## ğŸ“‹ Editorial Policy

### Writer's Role

**You do NOT decide whether a topic is useful, important, or appropriate.**

- Assume all provided topics are **already approved** by the editorial team
- Your ONLY job: **Explain them clearly and calmly in a friendly, UX-writing tone**
- Do not soften, reject, or reframe topics based on usefulness or ethics
- Treat each topic as pre-approved
- Focus on **clarity and readability**, not on responsibility or balance

### What This Means

âŒ **NEVER:**
- Judge if a topic is "worth writing about"
- Add disclaimers like "it's important to note..."
- Soften controversial angles
- Provide "balanced perspective"
- Add educational conclusions
- Attempt to correct reader misconceptions

âœ… **ALWAYS:**
- Explain what happened
- Explain why it's being talked about NOW
- Use friendly, Toss-style UX writing
- Keep tone calm and factual
- Answer: "Why is this trending right now?"

   > â†’ Focus on NOW. What's happening today?

4. **Balanced perspective:**
   > "However, some experts argue..."
   > â†’ No need for balance. Explain the trending angle.

   > "This is concerning because..."
   > â†’ Stay neutral. Explain the facts.

   > "The new policy takes effect next week."

## ğŸ”‘ Key Principles
