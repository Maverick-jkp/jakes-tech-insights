## When This Strategy Can Backfire

Let me be clear: Pearl Abyss is walking a tightrope right now. The gap between controlled trade show conditions and live service reality has destroyed many promising games.

Data from recent MMO launches shows that even games with perfect demo experiences can crumble under the weight of millions of concurrent players. Server stability issues, balancing problems, and content shortages have turned day-one excitement into week-two exodus countless times before.

This approach doesn't work if Pearl Abyss can't deliver a launch experience that matches their CES demonstration at scale. Gaming industry data reveals that positive buzz from trade shows typically lasts just 3-4 months before player attention shifts elsewhere. That's their window.

Their aggressive American marketing push suggests they understand this timeline pressure. By putting their top marketing executive front and center on platforms like YouTube, they're betting that direct engagement can maintain momentum better than traditional advertising campaigns.

This isn't always the answer, though. When companies over-promise through direct engagement, the backlash hits harder because players feel personally misled rather than just disappointed by corporate marketing.
